# Theory-of-Everything-Inspired Market Research Charter

## Long-Term Objective

Determine whether a conceptual framework about how reality evolves (a Theory of Everything, ToE) can be translated into a statistically valid and eventually economically useful model for financial markets.

This is **not** assumed to be true.

## Project Design Principles

This project is explicitly designed to:

- avoid self-deception
- avoid overfitting
- avoid narrative-driven conclusions
- prioritise falsifiability over success

## Conceptual Framework (ToE Summary)

The ToE proposes the following high-level ideas:

1. Systems evolve through configurations of relationships ("states"), not isolated objects.
2. Not all states are possible — constraints limit what configurations can exist.
3. Systems move through sequences of allowable states over time.
4. Periods of constraint (restricted behaviour) can lead to instability.
5. Instability may result in transitions to new states.
6. After transition, systems may expand into a broader range of behaviour.
7. Complex global patterns emerge from local interactions.
8. Outcomes are neither fully deterministic nor fully random — they arise from constrained possibilities.

## Application to Markets (Hypothesis Generation Only)

Markets are interpreted as dynamic systems where:

- "Constraint" corresponds to reduced price movement or restricted behaviour
- "Transition" corresponds to breakout events
- "Expansion" corresponds to increased price movement following a transition

This mapping is a hypothesis, not a fact.

## Critical Principle

The ToE is **not** being validated directly.

Instead:

- It is used only to generate testable hypotheses
- Every hypothesis must be expressed in measurable, quantitative terms
- Any idea that does not survive empirical testing is rejected

## Project Objective

The goal is **not** to prove the ToE correct.

The goal is:

Determine whether any hypothesis derived from this framework produces statistically significant, reproducible effects in market data that could later form the basis of a trading strategy.

## Methodological Rules

- Every claim must be testable
- Every variable must be measurable
- No optimisation before validation
- No parameter tuning after seeing results
- No post-hoc interpretation
- Simplicity is preferred over complexity
- Failure is an acceptable and expected outcome

## Important

This is a falsification-driven process.

The correct outcome of a step may be:

- "No effect exists under this definition"

That is considered success, not failure.

## Next Step

The next input will be a fully specified experimental protocol (Stage 1).

The implementation requirement is to execute it exactly, without modification.
